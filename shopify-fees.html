Shopify fees: growth gets expensive.

Shopify is a phenomenal storefront. But at standard online rates (around 2.9% + 30¢ per order on the entry plan, per Shopify's published pricing), processing quietly becomes one of your biggest line items as you grow. Every $10,000 in monthly sales costs roughly $320 in fees — $40,000/month is pushing $15,000 a year, before you count the extra penalty Shopify charges if you use an outside payment gateway instead of Shopify Payments.

Ways stores cut this down

  • Plan math. Higher Shopify plans carry lower card rates — sometimes the upgrade pays for itself on processing alone. Most owners never run this math; we will.
  • Retail/in-person volume. If you also sell in person (pop-ups, showroom, counter), that volume often shouldn't be running at online rates at all.
  • B2B and high-ticket orders. Large orders are where a percentage point matters most, and where alternative setups (invoicing, level-2/3 data, ACH for wholesale) save real money.
Honest caveat: for a pure-online store doing modest volume, Shopify Payments is often the right answer, and we'll tell you so. The wins are in mixed online/in-person businesses, high volume, and high average tickets.
