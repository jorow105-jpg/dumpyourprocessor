Square fees feel high? They usually are — past a certain size.

Square is a great way to start taking cards: no monthly fee, simple hardware, instant setup. But its simplicity has a price — a flat rate on every single tap and swipe (typically around 2.6% + 10¢ in person and higher online, per Square's published pricing). That flat rate doesn't care that a debit card costs the network a fraction of what a rewards credit card does. You pay the same either way, and Square keeps the difference.

When Square starts costing you real money

Under roughly $5,000/month in card volume, Square is honestly hard to beat. Above it, the math flips. At $20,000/month, a flat 2.6% + 10¢ means roughly $6,500+ per year in processing. On interchange-plus pricing — where you pay the network's actual cost plus a small transparent markup — the same volume often runs meaningfully less, especially if you take a lot of debit cards.

Rule of thumb: if you process more than $10k/month on Square, a statement review usually finds savings. If you're under $5k/month, we'll likely tell you to stay on Square — and we will.

What switching actually looks like

  • Keep selling the same day. A modern POS (free, included) replaces the Square reader — items, categories, and reporting come with it.
  • No contract. Square doesn't lock you in, and neither do we. No termination fees either direction.
  • Transparent pricing. You see the network's cost and our markup separately, on every statement.
